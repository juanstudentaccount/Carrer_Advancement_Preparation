Why this stakeholder was selected

I selected to interview Joseph because he works in automation controls and in his job he designs programs, implements and troubleshoots automated systems. This is valuable because he works in teams and manages important complex projects that require careful planning and team work strategies and because of this he can provide me with valuable real life examples of how he uses project management skills in his everyday work. Additionally Joseph is currently studying cybersecurity and his goal is to work in the field which is a common goal we share. His valuable insights can help me understand what employers look for in candidates and provide me with valuable insights on how to progress in my professional career.

üìù Conversation Summary

During my conversation with Joseph Cortez who is a senior Controls Automation engineer we talked about various important topics. In the beginning of our conversation I first started off by introducing myself where I explained that I'm a CIS student at Cal state LA, I then proceeded by introducing my semester long project which was my career advancement preparation charter. I explained that my goal is to secure an entry level IT help desk position by working on my deliverables. I then explained what my deliverables were and why they assist me with my goal and provide me with value for my professional career. After presenting my project I asked Joseph the first question which is how does he use project management in his life and daily work. Joseph responded by stating that project management is a mindset more than just a set of tools, he emphasized that in order to achieve any long term goal, whether it be in career, fitness or finances you need to start by having a clear objective and milestones that can guide you step by step, he gave examples from his own life such as his fitness journey where he set a step by step approach by counting his calories and protein intake and by using a workout structure. The second real life example he provided was his finances since he plans to save $20,000 every year. He also explained how he is using the same approach during his transition into cybersecurity and plans to use the same mindset. By identifying the skills he needs, planning how to acquire them and also using his technical background as an advantage.

When talking about his career Joseph explained how he uses project management as a senior controls and automation engineer. He described working on a $10 million dollar microchip construction project where he had to coordinate with a team and handle daily meetings, and constantly adapt to setbacks, like missing parts, scheduling conflicts, and unexpected issues. He highlighted the importance of daily checkins, adjusting plans in response to new information and always keeping the end goal in mind. Hearing these examples helped me understand how the project management principals I'm learning about are applied at a much larger scale in different industries. Finally, Joseph shared advice for me and my classmates who are learning project management. He said that the two most important traits for a project manager are strong organization and the ability to connect with people. He also emphasized that being organized prevents rework, saves time, and makes you more effective. Additionally he stated that building good relationships with your team makes people want to work with you and improves overall project outcomes. He also talked about what he looks for when working with people which is having a balance of competence and likability, having a good mindset, reliability and a positive attitude. Joseph also recommended the book How to Win Friends and Influence People by Dale Carnegie to develop interpersonal skills and he also encouraged me to not be afraid to ask for help or seek mentors. After finishing the interview I felt more confident about learning the importance of project management and how it's used in real life situations. I also gained important knowledge of why it's important to have a good mindset, organization, teamwork and networking skills and I will use this knowledge in my path to my future career.
